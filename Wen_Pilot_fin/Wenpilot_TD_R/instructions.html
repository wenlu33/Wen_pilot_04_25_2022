Instruction for Pilot Experiment

Welcome to the ExCEN laboratory and thanks for participating in this experiment!

You are about to participate in a session on decision-making, and you will be paid for your participation in cash at the end of the session. You will be paid in private and have no obligation to tell others how much you earned. At the end of the experiment, the points that you have earned will be converted in USD at the exchange rate of 100 points = 1 USD. You will receive $5 USD for your participation today. In addition, you can earn up to $14 USD depending on the actions each round.

You will have to answer all assessment about this instruction to continue with the experiment. Please read carefully, if at any time you have any questions, raise your hand and experimenter will come to you to answer it.

General rules

There are 8 subjects participating in this experiment.
The experiment will be repeated for 7 rounds.
At the beginning of each round, you will be randomly and anonymously assigned with a subject. For example, at the beginning of the first round, 4 pairs will be formed randomly and anonymously and in next round, another different 4 pairs will be formed and so on. In each round, you and the other subjectâ€™s choices will determine the amount earned. The results of you and the other subject will be communicated at the end of each round. At the end of the experiment, the earnings of each participant will be determined as the sum of their earnings of the 7 rounds.

Treatment with regret rules (this is for reading instruction across treatments, ONLY at pilot experiment)

In each round, you and the other subject will have to submit an integer between 40 and 200.

  • If the numbers submitted are the same, you will both earn points equal to the number submitted.
  • If the numbers submitted are different, you will both earn points equal to the lowest of the submitted numbers, plus or minus adjustment.
    • If the number you have submitted is smaller than the other subject's, you will earn 40 points and the other subject will lose 40 points.
    • If the number you have submitted is larger than the other subject's, you will lose 40 points and the other subject will earn 40 points.
